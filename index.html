Nonverbal Speech Recognition

Abstract

Nonverbal vocalizations, such as laughter and fillers, play a critical role in expressive speech generation and understanding, yet they are largely ignored in existing ASR and TTS systems. This limitation is particularly evident for low-resource languages like Vietnamese, where no dataset explicitly models nonverbal signals. In this work, we introduce Vinonverbal, the first Vietnamese dataset designed for nonverbal-aware ASR. The data are collected from real-world sources and processed through a semi-automatic pipeline, including speech separation, segmentation, and quality filtering, followed by ASR-based pseudo-labeling of nonverbal events. The resulting dataset contains approximately 40,000 Vietnamese samples (X hours of audio) with X types of nonverbal labels. We fine-tune multilingual ASR models, including Qwen3-ASR and Whisper, with extended nonverbal tokens. Evaluation on a manually annotated subset demonstrates effective recognition of nonverbal events while maintaining stable word error rate (WER).
